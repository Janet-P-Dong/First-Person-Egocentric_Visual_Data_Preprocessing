## Central Literature Job

The literature review should not ask only whether caregiving matters. That question is already well supported. The review should establish a sharper problem: responsive caregiving is developmentally important, but its effects are heterogeneous, context-sensitive, and likely conditional on the child's capacity to participate in, take up, and reorganize dyadic support.

## Chapter-Level Argument

1. Responsive caregiving and scaffolding are established developmental contexts.
2. Transactional and dynamic-systems theories make child participation central, not secondary.
3. Joint attention provides an observable behavioral route into child participation.
4. Responding to joint attention (RJA) and initiating joint attention (IJA) should be treated as distinct pathways.
5. Heterogeneity in caregiving effects is theoretically meaningful and should be modeled rather than averaged away.
6. Parent-child neural organization extends behavioral models, but synchrony alone cannot answer direction-of-influence questions.
7. Directionality and leadership models are needed to test whether dyads are parent-led, child-led, or reciprocally organized.
8. Longitudinal recalibration connects short-timescale dyadic organization with developmental change.
9. Similar designs show methodological precedent, but the dissertation's contribution is the integration of caregiving, joint attention, neural directionality, and change in one framework.

## Literature Review Spine

### Section 1. Responsive Caregiving Is a Developmental Context

Purpose: Establish the consensus. Responsive caregiving, sensitivity, scaffolding, and interaction quality are consistently linked with children's developmental outcomes. This section should be brief and selective. It should not become a full review of parenting.

Working claim:

> Responsive caregiving provides a social-learning context in which children receive, coordinate with, and potentially benefit from adult support.

Transition:

> The remaining question is not whether caregiving matters, but why its developmental effects are uneven.

### Section 2. Transactional Development Requires Child Participation

Purpose: Shift the model from parent input to dyadic organization. Dynamic and transactional perspectives imply that children's behavior changes the meaning and effectiveness of caregiving.

Working claim:

> If development is transactional, caregiving should not be modeled only as a stable parent-level exposure; it should be modeled as a dyadic process whose function depends on the child's current developmental organization.

Transition:

> To test this idea, child participation needs observable behavioral indicators.

### Section 3. Joint Attention Operationalizes Child Participation

Purpose: Introduce joint attention as the behavioral system through which children coordinate with caregiver support.

Working claim:

> Joint attention provides a measurable bridge between social interaction and learning because it captures how children orient to, share, and direct attention with another person.

Transition:

> However, responding to another person's attention and initiating shared attention are not the same developmental act.

### Section 4. RJA and IJA Mark Different Calibration Pathways

Purpose: Justify the dissertation's two-pathway design.

Working claim:

> RJA indexes the child's capacity to take up caregiver bids, whereas IJA indexes the child's capacity to recruit the caregiver into a shared attentional field.

Implication:

> Caregiving may become effective through different pathways depending on whether the child is primarily receiving support or actively reorganizing the interaction.

### Section 5. Heterogeneity Is the Target Phenomenon

Purpose: Defend nonlinear/localized analyses.

Working claim:

> Average caregiving effects may obscure developmental regions where the caregiving-child outcome pathway is active, weak, or reversed.

Implication:

> Piecewise and localized models are not merely exploratory conveniences; they are analytic tools aligned with a dynamic developmental claim.

### Section 6. Dyadic Neural Organization Extends the Behavioral Model

Purpose: Bring in hyperscanning/fNIRS without letting neural language overtake the behavioral theory.

Working claim:

> Parent-child interaction can be neurally organized, but synchrony alone does not specify whether organization is parent-led, child-led, or reciprocal.

Transition:

> A transactional model therefore needs directionality, not only coupling strength.

### Section 7. Directionality Tests the Leadership Question

Purpose: Justify MVGC and parent-to-child/child-to-parent indices.

Working claim:

> Directionality analyses provide temporally ordered estimates of influence that are better aligned with transactional theory than synchrony alone.

Interpretive boundary:

> Directionality should be interpreted as evidence of temporal organization, not as definitive causal proof.

### Section 8. Longitudinal Recalibration Connects Interaction to Development

Purpose: Connect immediate dyadic organization to developmental change.

Working claim:

> Developmental calibration should not be static; the functional meaning of caregiving, child attention, and neural organization may change as children move across the social-attention landscape.

### Section 9. Methodological Precedent and Dissertation Contribution

Purpose: Show that the project is feasible and situated, then name the integration.

Working claim:

> Prior studies provide strong component evidence for caregiving, joint attention, hyperscanning, and developmental change; the dissertation's contribution is to integrate these components into a calibrated model of when caregiving becomes functionally linked to child outcomes.

## Claim Language to Use

- Prior research supports...
- This literature suggests...
- These findings are consistent with...
- The present dissertation extends this work by...
- Directionality analyses estimate temporally ordered influence...
- Developmental calibration refers to the possibility that caregiving effects vary by child state, dyadic organization, and developmental timing.

## Claim Language to Avoid

- This proves that caregiving causes...
- No prior work has examined...
- Neural synchrony shows the mechanism...
- Directionality proves who caused whom...
- IJA and RJA are interchangeable indicators of joint attention.
