# Construct Language for Semantic Mapping

This note patches the core construct language for later BERT, BERTopic, manual coding, mediation interpretation, and dissertation writing.

## Core Reframe

Avoid repeatedly describing RIFL or related mediation findings as a generic "caregiving effect." That phrase is too broad and too causal-flat for what the dissertation is trying to show.

Preferred framing:

- developmental scaffolding
- translation of parental developmental support
- effective scaffolding
- uptake of parental scaffolding
- functional translation of parental support
- parent-to-child developmental support translation
- child uptake of contingent support
- scaffolding-to-outcome translation

## Why This Matters

RIFL is not simply measuring whether caregiving exists or whether parents have a global positive effect. It assesses how parental support is enacted in interaction:

- responsiveness to child signals,
- following the child's lead,
- contingent support,
- learning-oriented interaction quality,
- adjustment of adult behavior to the child's current developmental state.

The mediation models are therefore better interpreted as testing whether child agency and parent-child interaction processes help explain when parental support becomes developmentally functional.

## BERT and Topic-Modeling Implication

When searching, embedding, labeling topics, or constructing seed terms, include both broad caregiving terms and the more precise scaffolding/translation vocabulary.

Broad caregiving terms remain useful for recall:

- responsive caregiving
- parental responsiveness
- maternal responsiveness
- caregiver sensitivity
- parent sensitivity
- caregiver responsiveness
- responsive interaction

Scaffolding and translation terms should guide interpretation and topic labels:

- developmental scaffolding
- effective scaffolding
- parental scaffolding
- caregiver scaffolding
- contingent scaffolding
- guided participation
- learning support
- cognitive stimulation
- uptake of parental support
- uptake of parental scaffolding
- translation of parental support
- functional translation
- parent-child support translation

## Suggested Topic Labels

Use these labels when they fit the empirical cluster:

- Developmental Scaffolding
- Functional Translation of Parental Support
- Uptake of Parental Scaffolding
- Contingent Learning Support
- Child Agency in Support Uptake
- RIFL and Responsive Scaffolding

## Interpretation Rule

For RIFL-related findings, write "parental support is translated into child developmental gains" or "children differ in their uptake of parental scaffolding" rather than "caregiving affects children."
