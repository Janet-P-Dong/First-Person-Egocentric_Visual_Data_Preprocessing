# Chapter 1 Search Plan

## Development as a Dynamic System

Chapter 1 answers the question:

```text
How does development occur?
```

The purpose of this search is not to find measures or study-specific papers first. The purpose is to establish the theoretical and methodological problem that motivates the Developmental Calibration Framework.

## Core Argument

Developmental theory is dynamic, relational, and process-oriented, but much empirical research remains static, linear, average-effect oriented, and unidirectional.

The chapter should establish four claims:

1. Development emerges through reciprocal child-environment relations.
2. Development is not reducible to parenting, biology, or child traits alone.
3. Empirical methods often underspecify reciprocity, heterogeneity, nonlinear change, and reorganization.
4. A developmental calibration framework is needed to connect dynamic theory with empirical modeling.

## Recommended Search Order For Chapter 1

1. Transactional models and bidirectional parent-child effects.
2. Dynamic systems theory and nonlinear developmental change.
3. Developmental systems / relational developmental systems.
4. Developmental cascades.
5. Sociocultural scaffolding and guided participation.
6. Methodological gap searches: linearity, average effects, heterogeneity, and unidirectionality.

## What To Extract From Papers

For each landmark or review paper, extract:

- definition of development,
- assumptions about directionality,
- assumptions about child agency,
- assumptions about caregiver/environment role,
- whether development is framed as linear or nonlinear,
- whether individual differences are central or peripheral,
- methodological critique,
- how it supports the Developmental Calibration Framework.

## Chapter 1 Synthesis Claim

The chapter should end with this bridge:

```text
Developmental theory has long described development as reciprocal, dynamic, heterogeneous, and context-dependent. Yet empirical research often tests these ideas using linear, average-effect, and unidirectional models. The Developmental Calibration Framework is proposed to close this theory-method gap by examining where effects occur, for whom they occur, how parent-child systems are organized, and how they reorganize over time.
```
